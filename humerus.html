Diagnosis

Initial clinical examination and imaging for suspected proximal humerus fractures.

Initial assessment of proximal humerus fractures involves:

  • Clinical examination
  • Radiographs: AP and Neer views
  • Classification: Distraction vs. impaction injuries

Further Diagnostics

Concepts to consider when assessing proximal humerus fractures:

  • Varus/Valgus impaction
  • Calcar height
  • Part fractures

Further imaging such as CT may be needed to fully understand the fracture morphology and plan treatment.

Treatment Options

Treatment options for proximal humerus fractures include conservative and surgical approaches based on specific criteria:

  • Conservative: Non-displaced or minimally displaced fractures
  • Surgical:
    • ORIF: Indicated for displacements >1cm or angulation >45Â°, most varus-impacted fractures
    • Prosthesis: Considered for osteoporotic bone or complex fractures in elderly patients

Aftercare

Post-treatment care for proximal humerus fractures involves:

  • Rehabilitation protocols
  • Follow-up radiographs at 1 and 6 weeks
  • Physical therapy to restore function
